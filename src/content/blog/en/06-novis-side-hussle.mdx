<h1>My Side Hustle Gave Me Real Hustle</h1>

<h2>üò´ The Hard Stop</h2>

<p>

    That itself is a problem.<br/>
    Not because I can't function without him ‚Äî I can.<br/>
    But when machines break, Kernel is the kind of person who can fix them easily.<br/>

<p>
    This morning, I was stitching.<br/>
    Calm stitching.<br/>
    The good kind.<br/>
    The foot pedal kept a steady rhythm.<br/>
    The needle dipped in and out like it knew exactly where it belonged.<br/>
    Cotton thread pulled tight, then rested ‚Äî like a breath taken at the right moment.

<p>
    These pouches matter.<br/>
    They're part of my premium branding service ‚Äî hand-stitched, tactile, personal.<br/>
    The kind of thing people touch twice before putting away.

<p>
    A client wants exactly 100 of them for an event tomorrow morning.<br/>
    Not 98.<br/>
    Not "around a hundred."<br/>
    One. Zero. Zero.
</p>

<p>
    And then‚Äî<br/>
    The machine stopped.<br/>
    Not politely.<br/>
    Not with a warning sound.<br/>
    It jammed.

<p>
    The needle froze mid-dip, as if it had suddenly changed its mind about existence.<br/>
    The belt made a weak, offended noise.<br/>
    I reached for the hand wheel ‚Äî my comfort ‚Äî and it wouldn't move.
</p>

<p>
    I oiled it.<br/>
    Tapped it.<br/>
    Restarted it.<br/>
    Nothing.

<p>I stared at the half-stitched pouch trapped under the needle, like it had betrayed me personally.</p>

<p>
    That's when my phone rang.<br/>
    Client.<br/>
    "Just checking ‚Äî everything is on track, right?"
</p>

<p>
    I looked at the unfinished stack beside me.<br/>
    Clean numbers are cruel.
</p>

<p>
    "Yes," I said.<br/>
    My voice sounded braver than my wrists felt.
</p>

<p>
    After the call, panic stopped being abstract.<br/>
    It became physical ‚Äî tight fingers, shallow breath, the urge to shake the machine like it could be scared into cooperation.
</p>

<p>
    The local repair guy said,<br/>
    "Madam, this model is too old."
</p>

<p>
    Offline stores shook their heads.<br/>
    One shopkeeper smelled like grease and rust. He wiped his hands on a rag and said,<br/>
    "Parts are unavailable. We'll need to order them for manufacturing. Two to three weeks."
</p>

<p>
    Another didn't even hesitate.<br/>
    "Automatic only. No manual."
</p>

<p>
    One brightened briefly.<br/>
    "We have automatic with a manual handle. Default is automatic. You just pull this clutch before using."
</p>

<p>Tomorrow morning echoed in my head.</p>

<p>
    In the middle of all this, my phone rang again.<br/>
    Aunt Sonar.<br/>
    "Where are you? Your house is locked from outside," she said.<br/>
    "You remember you were supposed to give binary lessons to Codex and Enigma? They have an exam tomorrow. Hope you didn't forget."
</p>

<p>
    I had forgotten.<br/>
    Completely.
</p>

<p>If I didn't rush back home, Aunt Sonar would declare me unreliable ‚Äî publicly, dramatically, and forever. Possibly with witnesses.</p>

<p>
    I went home.<br/>
    Settled the kids down.<br/>
    Aunt Sonar left, satisfied but suspicious.
</p>

<p>
    I opened my laptop and searched again, just in case.<br/>
    Delivery dates laughed out loud.<br/>
    Then suggested I relax.
</p>

<p>
    From the corner of the room, Enigma said,<br/>
    "We have a test on binary addition tomorrow."
</p>

<p>
    I sighed.<br/>
    Reluctantly, I pulled out my whiteboard.
</p>

<p>
    The machine was broken.<br/>
    The order was exact.<br/>
    Time had no sympathy.<br/>
    And somehow, I was about to teach binary addition.
</p>

<h2>üëá The Workaround Nobody Asked For</h2>

<p>I wrote:</p>
<div class="font-mono text-xl my-4 p-4 bg-neutral-light border-l-4 border-status-success">

    0 + 1 = 1 <br/>
    1 + 0 = 1 <br/>

<div class="font-mono text-xl my-4 p-4 bg-neutral-light border-l-4 border-status-success">

<p>

<p><strong>Conversion Method</strong></p>
<div class="font-mono text-xl my-4 p-4 bg-neutral-light border-l-4 border-status-success">

<p>
    "I already taught you this method then," I continued, writing as I spoke.<br/>
    "So this is not new. This is just using it."

<p>The board filled up again, slower this time.</p>
<div class="font-mono text-xl my-4 p-4 bg-neutral-light border-l-4 border-status-success">
    110 = 1x2¬≤ + 1x2¬π + 0x2‚Å∞ = 6<br/>
    111 = 1x2¬≤ + 1x2¬π + 1x2‚Å∞ = 7

<div class="font-mono text-xl my-4 p-4 bg-neutral-light border-l-4 border-status-success">

<p>"And then you convert back."</p>
<div class="font-mono text-xl my-4 p-4 bg-neutral-light border-l-4 border-status-success">

<p>
    "That's it," I said.<br/>
    "That's what the book is doing."
</p>

<p>I stepped aside so they could see the whole thing at once.</p>

<p>
    "It's slower," I added, almost apologetically.<br/>
    "But it works."
</p>

<p>
    I tore a page from my notebook and slid it toward them.<br/>
    "Do a few on your own," I said.<br/>
    "Use the same steps. Convert, add, convert back."
</p>

<p>
    The room went quiet except for pencil scratches and whispered powers of two.<br/>
    I leaned against the table.<br/>

</p>

<p>
    The shopkeeper.<br/>
    "Default is automatic. You just need to pull this clutch."
</p>

<p>
    I looked at the board again.<br/>
    Not elegant.<br/>
    Not direct.<br/>
    But usable.
</p>

<p>
    I checked the time.<br/>
    Tomorrow morning.<br/>
    Exactly 100.
</p>

<p>
    I grabbed my bag.<br/>
    "Keep practicing," I said, already moving toward the door.<br/>
    "I'll be back."
</p>

<p>
    I wasn't fixing the old machine.<br/>
    I was going to buy the one that would work.
</p>

<h2>ü§ì The Direct Way</h2>

<p>
    I picked up the notebook.<br/>

    Everything was correct.<br/>
    Six-digit, seven-digit numbers. Painfully correct.
</p>

<p>
    I shook my head.<br/>
    "Okay," I said, "can I teach you a better method?"
</p>

<p>
    Codex stiffened immediately.<br/>
    "But it's not in the syllabus."
</p>

<p>"It will be faster," I said.</p>

<p>
    Enigma frowned.<br/>
    "But it will be difficult. Exam is tomorrow."
</p>

<p>
    I paused.<br/>
    Then said, slowly,<br/>
    "What if I tell you‚Ä¶ it's not new?"
</p>

<p>They looked at me.</p>

<p>"What if I say you already know it?"</p>

<p>
    "It's the same maths you've been doing in decimal," I said.<br/>
    "Nothing extra. Just realised differently."
</p>

<p>
    I pulled the whiteboard closer.<br/>
    "Forget conversion for a moment. Just add."
</p>

<p>I wrote:</p>
<div class="font-mono text-xl my-4 p-4 bg-neutral-light border-l-4 border-status-success">
&nbsp;&nbsp;110<br/>+ 111<br/>-------<br/>-------
</div>

<p>
    "Start from the right. Same as always."<br/>
    I pointed. "0 + 1 = 1."<br/>
    I wrote 1.

<p>
    "Next column. 1 + 1 = ?"

    "1 (carry) + 1 + 1 = 3."

<p>They paused.</p>

<div class="font-mono text-xl my-4 p-4 bg-neutral-light border-l-4 border-status-success">

</div>

<p>"1."</p>

</p>
